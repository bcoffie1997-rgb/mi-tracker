# MBDA Business Center Contact List — Status as of May 2026
Minority Business Development Agency (U.S. Department of Commerce)

## ⚠️ READ THIS FIRST — Category status is materially impaired

This is a *very different* category from APEX, VBOC, and SBDC. Before adding any of these as pipeline contacts, understand the current state:

**Timeline of events:**
- **March 14, 2025** — Executive Order directing MBDA (and several other agencies) to "be pared back to their statutory function." (Sources: Senate Commerce Committee, Inc., Small Business Majority.)
- **April 10, 2025** — Nearly every MBDA federal employee was let go or reassigned. Where MBDA once had ~100 employees, the agency now has "no more than one politically appointed staffer remaining" (Small Business Majority, May 12, 2025).
- **April 17, 2025** — Termination letters sent to MBDA grantees and Business Centers cancelling cooperative agreements (the Louisiana, North Carolina, and several other centers confirmed receipt that day).
- **Late April/early May 2025** — A Rhode Island Federal District Court preliminary injunction halted implementation of the executive order, and some termination letters were *rescinded*. But the practical operations remained frozen.
- **June 4, 2025** — Wisconsin MBDA Business Center announced it was "officially back in operation, at least through June 30, 2025, following the release of previously frozen federal funds" — with continued operation pending fifth-year approval.
- **June 26, 2025** — Senate Commerce Committee Democrats sent formal oversight letter to SBA Administrator Loeffler and Commerce Secretary Lutnick demanding answers; noted that the MBDA Business Centers program is **statutorily authorized under 15 U.S.C. § 9523** (so funding cancellation is legally contested).
- **President's FY 2026 budget proposes eliminating the MBDA entirely** along with Women's Business Centers, SCORE, STEP, and other entrepreneurial-development programs.
- **October 1, 2025** — Federal funding lapse began; Grants.gov shows MBDA Rural Business Center Program in limbo.

The current 2021 cohort of cooperative agreements was scheduled to run through **June 30, 2026** — so for the next ~6 weeks, the centers that still have active funding will continue operating, but the renewal is highly uncertain.

**What this means for MI Enterprise outreach:**

1. **Most centers cannot commit to multi-year SaaS contracts right now.** Their own funding is on a month-to-month basis pending political and legal resolution. A 12-month annual contract is hard to justify when you don't know if you'll exist in 60 days.
2. **But the human relationships are still gold.** The program directors at these centers are deep experts in minority federal contracting, have networks of hundreds of MBE clients, and many will land at *other* organizations (state ED agencies, chambers, supplier-development councils, consulting firms) where they'll still need procurement intelligence.
3. **Strategy recommendation:** Treat MBDA contacts as **Tier 3 / nurture-only** until the funding picture stabilizes. Don't lead with enterprise pricing. Lead with relationship — offer pilot access, content, or LinkedIn connection. They'll remember.
4. **Backdoor opportunity:** The *operators* (host orgs) of MBDA centers are nearly all longstanding minority supplier development councils, chambers, universities, and ED agencies — they're enterprise buyers regardless of whether the MBDA contract continues. Pitch the host org, not the MBDA program.

## Recommendations

### Don't lead pricing conversations here right now
- Move all MBDA contacts to **Tier 3 / nurture** status in your tracker.
- Do not include MBDA centers in your "90-day enterprise pipeline targets."
- Do continue building relationships, offering content, and tracking these directors on LinkedIn — they'll land somewhere else if their center closes.

### Focus on host orgs that exist independently of MBDA funding
The MSDCs (Minority Supplier Development Councils) — ChicagoMSDC, DFW MSDC, Detroit/Michigan MSDC, Rocky Mountain MSDC, Florida MSDC, GNEMSDC, NCMSDC, Capital Region MSDC — are member organizations with **independent revenue** from corporate members. They exist regardless of MBDA funding and serve hundreds of MBEs each. These are real enterprise buyers.

Same for the universities (Georgia Tech, UTSA, U of Hawaii, Houston CC, USC, Southern University) — they have institutional budgets.

### Highest-leverage MBDA-adjacent accounts (if you pursue this category)
1. **Georgia Tech Enterprise Innovation Institute** — hosts MBDA Atlanta (Donna Ennis) AND Georgia Tech APEX (Nancy Cleveland) under one roof.
2. **UTSA** — hosts San Antonio MBDA AND UTSA APEX AND South-West Texas SBDC. Three federal contracting programs, one campus.
3. **Houston Community College** — Houston MBDA (Marshette Turner). UH (separate) hosts UH APEX and Texas Gulf Coast SBDC. Houston is a top-3 metro for this category.
4. **The Enterprise Center, Philadelphia** — Rendell Bradley. Larger CDFI plus MBDA combination.

### Watch list (track political/legal developments)
- Rhode Island Federal District Court injunction case
- FY 2026 final appropriations (will determine if MBDA survives)
- Any new NOFO releases from MBDA in 2026 — would indicate the program is being restored

## Caveats
- Several center directors not confirmed in this research round due to MBDA.gov being inaccessible to web-fetch and stale records on the archive site. For any MBDA contact you import, verify current employment status before outreach — many centers have closed, paused, or laid off staff in 2025.
- Funding numbers cited (e.g. $410,000/year) reflect the 2021 NOFO. Whether full Year 5 was disbursed in 2025-2026 varies by center and is the subject of ongoing litigation.
- The El Paso center closure (July 2024) and Tacoma closure are *separate* events from the broader 2025 funding crisis — predates it.
- The MBDA Rural Business Center Program NOFO posted in early 2025 (deadline March 17, 2025) is currently in funding-lapse limbo per Grants.gov.
